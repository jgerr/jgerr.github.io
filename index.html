Greetings and good day!

I'm Joanna Gerr, currently a sophomore in the class of 2020 at MIT.

At MIT, I study Game Design in the Comparative Media Studies department, and am minoring in Computer Science. Though game design is my passion, I believe that understanding game development is an essential component to being truly adept at designing games.

To me, video games are a form of interactive art; the medium allows you to experience and influence a story, as opposed to just making you spectate predetermined events. Games that can impart meaningful messages through play are my favorite kinds of games.

I'm driven by the desire to design captivating, complex games that will impact others as profoundly as they have impacted me. Maybe it's a ways off in the future, but I'll work hard until I get there!

You can reach me at gerr@mit.edu.

Art & Design
LINE

I'm an active content creator for the messaging app LINE. Pictured here is part of Daily Lives of Ice Cream, my sticker pack that features delicious ice cream cones in their daily lives.

Crafting sticker packs around a cohesive theme is a fun pastime for me. As a details-oriented person, it was thrilling to plan out ways for these ice cream cones to convey 16 different emotions given the affordances of their basic anatomy.

ES@B

I was commissioned to design the headers and backgrounds for ESports at Berkeley's Twitch.tv team streaming page.

I was told that the background needed reference the school and incorporate each of Berkeley's semi-pro college ESports teams. Thinking of the best way to satisfy all these constraints, I decided upon a collage-like background that could show off the faces of the ESports teams alongside a panoramic image of Berkeley's famed tower at sunset, which also fit Berkeley's orange-and-navy color scheme perfectly!

E-Book GIF Library

I created a collection of .gifs for E-Books that help children with learning disabilities expand their vocabulary. The full library features over 40 gifs ordered by subject matter, clustered around different definitions for a single word. These here show different representations of the word "find": to find land, to find a constellation, and to find treasure.

3D Modeling

One of my hobbies is designing 3D assets in Blender. Over time, I've been learning how to animate 3D characters and experimenting with the different types of stylistic effects one can achieve--like using cel shading to make assets look cartoon-like, or removing shadows to make 3D objects seem 2D.

Kaleidoscope

One field that has always interested me is computationally generated artwork. I figure that the best way to understand something is to experience it yourself, so as an experiment one summer, I created a javascript kaleidoscope using HTML5 Canvases.

Animation
Mr. Balloon's Wild Ride

In my senior year of highschool, my animated short Mr. Balloon's Wild Ride was the runner-up in the Virginia Film Festival's High School Directors competition.

Using TVPaint, everything in this animation was drawn or designed by my hand--even the musical accents! I took inspiration from the style of old silent films, splicing in black-and-white dialogue screens to help narrate the story of a balloon boy who lost his head.

Environmental PSA

My PSA won the top prize in the World of Seven Billion's Deforestation PSA competition.

Since I wanted this informational video to be reminiscient of infographics, I made it using vector graphics in Adobe Flash. When designing this PSA, I realized how heavily design is influenced by content. Because I needed to convey information both visibly and audibly, I flipped my usual workflow: I first recorded the PSA, and then animated to match the timing on the recorded track.

Fight Club

Games

Hibearnation

In Hibearnation, thirty distinct levels represent the thirty days leading up to winter. I developed this game from scratch using C++ in Unity, making all the pixel-art assets, generating the sound effects, designing the levels, scripting behaviours for different terrains, and implementing motion controls for the player character.

My favorite part of the process was thinking about how to design the levels such that they would increase in difficulty and keep the player engaged. In keeping with the theme, I decided to add more types of environmental interactions in later levels. This video shows Level 17, where you need to collect four veggies to clear the level, and traverse three different types of terrain: normal orange bricks, slowing white snow, and slippery blue ice.

Downboar

Downboar is a light bullet-hell style game that draws gameplay influence from the Touhou Project. The player gathers points by lightly grazing pellets of mud, trying to avoid a full on collision between the mud and their snouts. Downboar was also developed entirely by me in C++ with Unity.

Bullet hell patterns are an art form! I'm always in awe at the gorgeous structures Touhou spawning algorithms create, and I had great fun designing bullet shapes and patterns of my own. It was a thrill to create my own honing algorithm that target the player's weak spot, because I've died to plenty of those before. In this video, you can see how different bullet speeds bullets appear at intervals, and observe patterns like the V-shaped formations.
