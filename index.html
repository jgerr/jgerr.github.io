Nice to meet you!

One way or another, you've managed to stumble upon Joanna Gerr's portfolio. Welcome!

Well, who exactly is that? I'm currently a senior at Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology. As the categories on the left indicate, my main interests lie in Computer Science and Design-type fields. I've taken CompSci courses for all four years in which I've been at school, going beyond AP CompSci and into Artificial Intelligence and Web + Mobile Application Development, and culminating in my being a part of Thomas Jefferson's Computer Systems senior lab.

Animation is a field near and dear to me that I have pursued outside of my classes for many years. Having grown up on the animated films of Studio Ghibli, I developed a fondness for traditional frame-by-frame animation. Around the start of middle school, my parents gifted me with a copy of Adobe Flash CS 5.5 so that I could make my own. Since then, I've created two longer animations, over fifty .gif animations for e-books for disabled children learning to communicate, and numerous other short animations for fun.

Over the past fourteen years, I've learned to both play and love the piano. It's more than just simply practicing for competitions and drilling technique; the compositions of romantic composers as Debussy and Ravel and contemporary composers like Joe Hisaishi and Akira Yuyama have inspired me to create my own music.
