Relic Race
An adventerous match-3 game by Funkitron.
Relic Race
I interned for Funkitron as a Level Designer on their upcoming mobile match-3 game Relic Race in Summer 2018.

As a Level Designer, I learned the mechanics of Relic Race and built interesting levels by combining them in various patterns. In addition, I worked with the rest of the Level Design team to create cohesive level 'arcs' that introduced new mechanics, and balanced levels made by other interns.

In my time interning with Funkitron, I was also entrusted with the task of creating an animatic for Relic Race's intro cutscene. I worked together with the lead vision holder to write design docs fleshing out the introductory narrative, and then made several iterations upon the intro animatic for Relic Race as new constraints arose.

I also designed the Narrative Tip feature, wherein characters say short blurbs of flavor text relevant to the player's current progress along the main story line in between loading screens.

Lastly, I also aided in linking together several components of the game using Funkitron's system architecture, so that the right tasks would come after their intended dialogues, the right animations would play at the right times, and scenes would transition smoothly between sections of the story.

CLEVR
Research into Collaborative Learning Environments in Virtual Reality.
CLEVR
I did research with the MIT Game Lab and STEP Lab on CLEVR from Summer 2017 to Spring 2018.

CLEVR, or Collaborative Learning Environments in Virtual Reality, is an Oculus Rift project that aims to show the viability of VR as a means of education in classrooms. The underlying idea was to tackle a canonical topic in science that could be better represented through the use of VR tech.

The first summer, I worked with 5 other undergraduates to conceptualize and prototype a cellular biology-focused game that would enhance a student's understanding of the internal size and scale of a cell. As a generalist, I created 3D organelle models and 2D textures, wrote code to procedurally generate microtubules, and designed a number of movement schemes.

In the 2018 school year, I shifted focus to being a UI/UX designer, fleshing out the asynchronous aspect of our design. I designed several mockups for tablet tools that a player outside the VR environment would see and use to interact with the player inside of VR.

Inspectre
A heartwarming 2D puzzle-platformer about saving your uncle.
Inspectre
Inspectre is a 2D puzzle-platformer created by Team Momo for the class CMS.617. It's about a boy, Pierre, who has gone to look for his missing mad scientist uncle.

I was Team Momo's artist, and it was my job to handle all visual components of the game--from characters and their animations, to sprites for the various interactable objects littered throughout the environment, to static and parallaxed backgrounds, to UI components and beyond.

Hibearnation
A mobile game where you, a bear, prebear to hibearnate.
Hibearnation
Hibearnation is an isometric, dexterity-focused mobile game I created by myself in 2015.

The basic premise is: you're a bear stocking up on food before winter arrives. Get through 30 levels (read: days) to succeed!

In creating this, I designed mechanics that fit the theme and would allow for interesting gameplay, hand-crafted 30 different levels using these mechanics, and balanced them against friends and family.
